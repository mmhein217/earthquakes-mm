About the Earthquake

Understanding the tragedy that struck Myanmar on March 15, 2025

The Disaster

Key facts about the catastrophic event

March 15, 2025

Main Shock

A massive 7.8 magnitude earthquake struck central Myanmar at 8:17 AM local time. The epicenter was located 50 km northwest of Naypyidaw at a depth of 10 km.

March 15-17, 2025

Aftershocks

Over 120 significant aftershocks were recorded, including three above magnitude 6.0. These caused additional damage to already weakened structures.

March 16, 2025

International Response

The Myanmar government declared a state of emergency and requested international assistance. Rescue teams from neighboring countries began arriving.

March 20, 2025

Death Toll Rises

Confirmed fatalities surpassed 1,000 as rescue teams reached remote areas. An estimated 300,000 people were displaced from their homes.

Tectonic Cause

The earthquake occurred along the Sagaing Fault, a major transform fault between the Indian and Sunda plates that has produced several large historical earthquakes.

Structural Damage

Over 50,000 buildings were destroyed or severely damaged, including many older structures not built to earthquake-resistant standards.

Geological Effects

The quake caused significant ground deformation, with some areas experiencing up to 3 meters of horizontal displacement along the fault.

Human Impact

The toll on Myanmar's people and communities

Displacement

Over 300,000 people were forced to leave their homes, creating the largest internal displacement crisis in Myanmar's recent history. Temporary shelters were established in schools, monasteries, and open areas.

Medical Crisis

Hospitals were overwhelmed with thousands of injured. Many medical facilities suffered structural damage, forcing doctors to treat patients in tents and makeshift clinics.

Infrastructure

Critical infrastructure including roads, bridges, and utilities were severely damaged, hampering rescue efforts and isolating many communities for days after the quake.

Cultural Loss

Several historic pagodas and cultural heritage sites were damaged or destroyed, representing a significant loss to Myanmar's cultural heritage.

Response & Recovery

How Myanmar and the world responded

1

Immediate Rescue

Local communities initiated rescue efforts immediately, often using bare hands to dig through rubble in the critical first hours before heavy equipment arrived.

2

International Aid

Over 20 countries sent search-and-rescue teams, medical personnel, and relief supplies. The UN coordinated a global humanitarian response.

3

Temporary Shelter

Over 200 temporary shelters were established, providing housing, food, and medical care to displaced populations.

4

Rebuilding

A long-term reconstruction plan was developed with international partners, focusing on earthquake-resistant construction standards.

About This Memorial

Honoring those affected by the disaster

This memorial website was created to preserve the memory of those who lost their lives, to honor the survivors, and to recognize the heroic efforts of responders and volunteers.

Our mission is to provide accurate information about the disaster, document its impacts, and serve as a resource for those affected and those wishing to help with ongoing recovery efforts.

The memorial also serves as a call to action for improved earthquake preparedness in Myanmar and the surrounding region.
